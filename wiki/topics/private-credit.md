# Private Credit

Private credit is a non-bank lending market that provides debt financing outside public bond markets and outside traditional bilateral bank lending. The current material presents it as a rapidly growing market that offers customized financing and high yield, but also concentrates opacity, illiquidity, and refinancing risk.

## Core Ideas

- Private credit expanded after the financial crisis as tighter bank regulation, bank consolidation, and the rise of private companies left more financing demand outside traditional channels.
- Direct lending and asset-based finance are the two main durable modes in the current material.
- Borrowers value speed, tailoring, confidentiality, and committed capital more than the standardization of bank or public-market debt.
- Investors value high floating-rate income, long-duration assets, and low reported mark-to-market volatility.
- Asset-based finance increasingly reaches infrastructure-like collateral, including AI-related data-center financing, not just traditional middle-market corporate loans.
- Market growth increasingly depends on ties to private equity sponsors, insurance capital, banks, and structured funding channels.
- The main structural risks are weak price discovery, limited secondary liquidity, refinancing pressure, and hidden contagion through structured exposures.
