# Grounded Rewards

Grounded rewards are learning signals that come from measurable consequences in the environment rather than only from human prejudgement of candidate actions.

## Why It Matters

- Environmental feedback can reveal strategies that humans would not have specified or endorsed in advance.
- Grounded signals connect optimization to real outcomes such as health metrics, test results, cost, error rate, or scientific measurements.
- A grounded reward can still be user-steered by selecting or combining signals that correspond to the user's goal.

## Practical Tension

- A single metric can be misaligned or gamed, so reward design and adaptation remain central problems.
- User feedback may still be needed at a higher level to refine how grounded signals are interpreted.
- Long-horizon grounded rewards require better credit assignment and monitoring than short conversational tasks.
